About Skyrim Together Reborn

For years, Bethesda fans around the world have yearned for a multiplayer experience in their favourite games. Many felt as though ESO and Fallout76 simply didn't fit the bill, providing more of an MMO experience than a co-op one, and thus the Together Team was formed. Though originally focused on SkyrimTogether specifically, we have since expanded to include FalloutTogether as well. Since this is a passion project there are no promised release dates or estimates on when the mod will be complete. However, you can monitor our progress by checking the News tab or our social media.



To play Skyrim Together Reborn, you must use Skyrim Special Edition 1.6 on Steam. You do not need the Anniversary Upgrade paid mod pack. In fact, we recommend playing without it. The VR, console, Legendary and Gamepass editions are not supported. Neither is Skyrim Special Edition 1.5 (aka pre Anniversary update). To make sure you have the latest update of Special Edition installed, verify the game files through Steam.

How to install Skyrim Together Reborn

The below instructions are for those who are fairly experienced modders already. If you are not very good at modding or computers, we highly recommend you watch the installation video instead: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7ogIhWUxPyE
To install Skyrim Together Reborn, we recommend you use a mod manager like Vortex or Mod Organizer 2. The mod requires another mod to load, Addres Library. You need to download the All in one (Anniversary Edition) version. Use the "Mod Manager Download" button to install it with your mod manager of choice. You can download Reborn on nexus here. Once the mod is installed and enabled, you can find SkyrimTogether.exe in your Skyrim game folder. This can be found at Data/SkyrimTogetherReborn/SkyrimTogether.exe. You can either configure your mod manager to add a launch button for this executable, or you can create a desktop shortcut by right clicking it. When you launch the mod for the first time, it will most likely ask you to find SkyrimSE.exe. If it did not automatically find the correct folder and you do not know where this is, go to Steam, right click "The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Special Edition", click "Manage", and click "Browse local files". Copy the location of this over to the prompt.
